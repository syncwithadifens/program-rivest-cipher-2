What is Rivest Cipher 2?

In cryptography, RC2 (also known as ARC2) is a symmetric-key block cipher designed by Ron Rivest in 1987. "RC" stands for "Ron's Code" or "Rivest Cipher"; other ciphers designed by Rivest include RC4, RC5, and RC6.

RC2 (Rivest Cipher 2) is a block cipher designed by Ron Rivest in 1987. Development of RC2 was sponsored by Lotus which was looking for a specific cipher after evaluation by the NSA for export as part of the Lotus Notes software. After further negotiations, this cipher was approved and exported in 1989. As is known, the key restriction for cryptographic exports by the US government is only 40 bits. All of this is contained in the export regulation for cryptography. The details of this algorithm are kept strictly confidential and are the property of RSA Security. But on January 29, 1996, the source code for RC2 was published on the Internet by an anonymous person via the Usenet forum, namely Sci.crypt.

RC2 is a block cipher that uses 64 bits as the size per block with keys that vary in size (0-1024 bits). By changing the size of this key, the performance of RC2 can be two or three times better than DES (Data Encryption Standard), an algorithm developed by the NSA (National Security Agent) and designated as the standard encryption algorithm by the US government in 1976- 1997 (which was later superseded by AES, Advanced Encryption Standard).
